Research Interests

I am broadly interested in combinatorics (of an algebraic flavor), topology and geometry, and finite field theory. Tangentially, I am also interested in algorithm design and verification as well as developing software packages for use in mathematical research.

I spent Summer 2018 at the SMALL REU program at Williams College, where I worked in the tropical geometry group under the supervision of Ralph Morrison. Our group studied divisors on graphs from a combinatorial perspective and proved results relating various forms of an invariant known as the gonality of a graph. I presented my results at MathFest 2018 where I was recognized with an outstanding student presentation award.

In high school, I also worked on an independent research project under the supervision of Julie Rana. We studied roots of certain polynomials over finite fields and proved results regarding "linearity" of these roots in some special cases. I presented these results at the MAA NCS Meeting of Fall 2017.
